The Problem
Young adults are unprepared for healthcare decisions
Healthcare costs in the U.S. reached $5.3 trillion in 2024, yet college students lack the financial literacy to navigate health insurance and HSAs.

Research
What we learned
We used multiple methods to understand our users' needs, validate our assumptions, and inform the design.
üé§

User Interviews

N = 8 participants

Semi-structured interviews with UCI undergrads exploring their healthcare literacy, HSA awareness, and learning preferences.

üìã

Online Survey

N = 45 responses

Quantitative survey measuring current health insurance knowledge, HSA familiarity, and attitudes toward gamified learning.

üîç

Competitive Analysis

N = 6 platforms

Reviewed existing HSA education tools (Fidelity, HSA Bank, NerdWallet, etc.) for content gaps and UX patterns.

Key Findings
  • Low awareness: 78% of surveyed students could not define what an HSA is or distinguish it from an FSA
  • Terminology barrier: Terms like "deductible," "premium," and "HDHP" were cited as top sources of confusion in 6 of 8 interviews
  • Learning preference: 85% of respondents preferred interactive or scenario-based learning over reading articles or watching videos
  • Engagement gap: Existing HSA platforms are designed for 35+ professionals ‚Äî zero products target college-age users
  • Motivation: Students are more willing to learn when progress is tracked and achievements are visible
